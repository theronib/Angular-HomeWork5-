{{"The American Dream" | uppercase}}

The largest car in the world is the "American Dream," a colossal limousine created by renowned car customizer Jay Ohrberg. Originally constructed from six 1976 Cadillac Eldorado limousines, it measured an impressive 60 feet (18.28 meters) when it was first completed in 1986. However, Ohrberg later extended the vehicle to a staggering 100 feet (30.5 meters), making it the longest car in the world and earning it a spot in the Guinness Book of World Records.

{{"Antonov An-225 Mriya" | uppercase}}

The largest airplane in the world is the Antonov An-225 Mriya. This massive aircraft, originally designed to carry the Soviet Buran space shuttle, boasts a wingspan of 88.4 meters (290 feet) and a length of 84 meters (275 feet). The An-225 is capable of transporting oversized cargo and has a maximum takeoff weight of around 640,000 kg (1.4 million pounds). Only one An-225 was ever built, and it remains in service today, used primarily for transporting heavy and bulky loads​

{{"Icon of the Seas" | uppercase}}

As of 2024, the largest ship in the world is the "Icon of the Seas," a cruise ship by Royal Caribbean. This ship, which is set to embark on its maiden voyage in January 2024, measures an impressive 1,198 feet in length and can accommodate up to 7,600 passengers, along with 2,805 staterooms spread across its 20 decks. The ship weighs around 250,000 tons and features a variety of amenities, including the largest water park at sea, multiple pools, numerous dining options, and various themed neighborhoods designed to enhance the passenger experience​
